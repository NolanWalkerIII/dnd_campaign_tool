# Adventuring Rules

## Time

| Scale | Usage |
|-------|-------|
| Round | 6 seconds. Used in combat. |
| Minute | 10 rounds. Used for short activities (searching a room, casting a ritual). |
| Hour | Used for travel, exploration. |
| Day | Travel pace, resting, downtime. |

## Travel

### Travel Pace

| Pace | Distance/Hour | Distance/Day | Effect |
|------|--------------|-------------|--------|
| Fast | 4 miles | 30 miles | -5 passive Perception |
| Normal | 3 miles | 24 miles | — |
| Slow | 2 miles | 18 miles | Can use Stealth |

These assume 8 hours of travel per day. Traveling more than 8 hours requires a CON save (DC 10 + 1 per hour past 8) each extra hour or gain one level of exhaustion.

### Forced March
Each hour beyond 8, every creature must make the CON save above. Mounts and vehicles don't suffer exhaustion from forced march but have their own limits.

### Mounts and Vehicles

| Mount | Speed | Carrying Capacity |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| Riding Horse | 60 ft (gallop: 8 miles/hour for 1 hour, then must rest 1 hour) | 480 lb |
| Warhorse | 60 ft | 540 lb |
| Pony | 40 ft | 225 lb |
| Camel | 50 ft | 480 lb |
| Elephant | 40 ft | 1,320 lb |

Vehicles: Cart (2 mph), wagon (2 mph), rowboat (1.5 mph), sailing ship (2 mph), keelboat (1 mph), longship (3 mph), galley (4 mph).

### Navigation
In open terrain with landmarks: no check needed. In dense forest, swamp, mountains, or ocean: WIS (Survival) check to avoid becoming lost. DC depends on terrain and conditions (usually 10-15). Getting lost means the party travels in a random direction until someone realizes the mistake.

### Foraging
WIS (Survival) check during travel.
- DC 10: Abundant food and water (forests, plains)
- DC 15: Sparse resources (mountains, desert edges)
- DC 20: Very scarce (desert, arctic)

Success: 1d6 + WIS modifier pounds of food and/or gallons of water.

### Encounter Frequency
DM decides. Common approach:
- Wilderness: Roll d20 per hour or per 4-hour block. Encounter on 18+ (dangerous region) or 20 (safe road).
- Dungeon: Roll per room entered or per 10 minutes spent exploring.

## Resting

### Short Rest
- Duration: Minimum 1 hour of light activity (eating, reading, bandaging wounds, standing watch)
- **Hit Dice**: Spend one or more Hit Dice to heal. Roll each die + CON modifier, regain that many HP.
- **Recharges**: Some class features (Warlock spell slots, Fighter's Second Wind and Action Surge, Monk's Ki, Bard's Bardic Inspiration at level 5+, Channel Divinity, some subclass features)
- Frequency: No RAW limit. DM controls pacing by controlling when the party can rest.

### Long Rest
- Duration: Minimum 8 hours, with at most 2 hours of light activity (standing watch counts). At least 6 hours must be sleeping.
- **Regain**: All HP, half your total Hit Dice (rounded down, minimum 1), all spent spell slots, most class features.
- **Limit**: Can't benefit from more than one long rest per 24 hours.
- If interrupted by 1+ hours of strenuous activity (walking, fighting, casting spells), restart the 8-hour clock.

### Gritty Realism (Optional Rule)
Short rest = 8 hours. Long rest = 7 days. This dramatically changes resource management and makes every encounter feel more threatening. Works well for exploration-heavy or low-magic campaigns.

### Epic Heroism (Optional Rule)
Short rest = 5 minutes. Long rest = 1 hour. Party is always near full power. Good for one-shots or high-action sessions.

## Light and Vision

### Light Levels

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Bright light | Normal vision for all creatures |
| Dim light | Lightly obscured. Disadvantage on Perception checks relying on sight. |
| Darkness | Heavily obscured. Effectively blinded. |

### Darkvision
Treat darkness as dim light (within range). Treat dim light as bright light. Still have disadvantage on Perception in what is naturally darkness (because it becomes dim light, which is lightly obscured).

Darkvision doesn't let you see in magical darkness unless the ability specifically says so (Devil's Sight, for example).

### Light Sources

| Source | Bright | Dim | Duration |
|--------|--------|-----|----------|
| Candle | 5 ft | 5 ft | 1 hour |
| Torch | 20 ft | 20 ft | 1 hour |
| Lamp | 15 ft | 30 ft | 6 hours (per flask of oil) |
| Hooded Lantern | 30 ft | 30 ft | 6 hours |
| Bullseye Lantern | 60 ft cone | 60 ft | 6 hours |
| Light cantrip | 20 ft | 20 ft | 1 hour |
| Dancing Lights | 10 ft each (4 lights) | 10 ft each | 1 minute (concentration) |
| Continual Flame (2nd) | 20 ft | 20 ft | Until dispelled |
| Daylight (3rd) | 60 ft | 60 ft | 10 minutes |

### Obscurement

| Type | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Lightly obscured (dim light, patchy fog, moderate foliage) | Disadvantage on Perception checks relying on sight |
| Heavily obscured (darkness, opaque fog, dense foliage) | Effectively blinded |

## Food, Water, and Survival

### Food Requirements
- 1 lb of food per day per creature (Medium size)
- Going without: After days equal to 3 + CON modifier (minimum 1), gain 1 exhaustion level per day without food. A normal day of eating resets the count.

### Water Requirements
- 1 gallon of water per day (2 gallons in hot weather)
- Half ration: DC 15 CON save at end of day or gain 1 exhaustion (2 if already exhausted)
- No water: 1 exhaustion at end of day (2 if already exhausted). Can't be removed until the creature drinks its full requirement.

### Suffocation
Creature can hold breath for 1 + CON modifier minutes (minimum 30 seconds). When out of breath: survive for CON modifier rounds (minimum 1), then drop to 0 HP and is dying. Can't be stabilized or regain HP until it can breathe.

## Environment Hazards

### Falling
1d6 bludgeoning per 10 ft fallen, max 20d6 (200 ft). Land prone unless you avoid all damage.

### Extreme Cold
Below 0°F: DC 10 CON save each hour or gain 1 exhaustion. Creatures with cold resistance or immunity auto-succeed. Cold weather gear lets you auto-succeed.

### Extreme Heat
Above 100°F: DC 5 CON save each hour (+1 per additional hour, to a max of DC 15) or gain 1 exhaustion. Fire resistance/immunity auto-succeeds. Drinking water resets the DC.

### Strong Wind
Disadvantage on ranged weapon attacks and WIS (Perception) checks relying on hearing. Extinguishes open flames. Fog dispersed.

### Heavy Precipitation
Lightly obscures area. Disadvantage on Perception (sight). Extinguishes open flames.

### Frigid Water
Submerged: DC 10 CON save each minute or gain 1 exhaustion. Cold resistance/immunity auto-succeeds.

### Quicksand
Entering: Sink 1d4+1 feet. Each round, sink 1d4 feet. At depth = creature's height: completely submerged (treat as suffocating). Escape: Action, STR check DC 10 + depth in feet. Another creature can pull with same check at advantage.

### Slippery Ice
Difficult terrain. DC 10 DEX (Acrobatics) when moving or fall prone.

## Traps

### Detecting Traps
WIS (Perception) to spot a trap or its trigger. INT (Investigation) to determine its nature and how to disable it. DCs set by trap complexity:
- Simple: DC 10
- Moderate: DC 15
- Complex: DC 20

### Disarming Traps
DEX check using thieves' tools (or the appropriate tool). Same DC as detection, or higher. Failure by 5+ typically triggers the trap.

### Example Traps

**Pit Trap**: Hidden pit, 10 ft deep. DC 15 Perception to spot, DC 10 DEX save to avoid. 1d6 bludgeoning. Spiked variant: add 1d10 piercing.

**Poison Dart Trap**: Trigger plate fires darts. DC 15 Perception to spot trigger. +8 to hit, 1d4 piercing + 2d6 poison (DC 13 CON save for half poison).

**Poison Needle**: In a lock or chest. DC 20 Investigation to find. DC 15 DEX save or 1 piercing + 2d10 poison.

**Rolling Boulder**: Classic. DC 15 Perception to notice trigger. DC 15 DEX save or 4d10 bludgeoning.

**Collapsing Ceiling**: 4d10 bludgeoning, DC 15 DEX save for half. 10 ft square area becomes difficult terrain.

## Dungeon Exploration

### Dungeon Pace
Normal exploration: roughly 200 ft per minute (careful), 400 ft per minute (fast). These are rough guidelines — actual pace depends on what the party does (checking for traps, mapping, fighting).

### Doors
Stuck doors: DC 10-20 STR check to force. Locked: DC 10-20 thieves' tools or STR check to break.

### Secret Doors
WIS (Perception) or INT (Investigation) check. DC set by construction quality (usually DC 15-20). Passive Perception can reveal obvious ones.

### Underwater Exploration
See combat rules for holding breath. Dim light in clear water past 60 ft. Murky water is heavily obscured past 10 ft.

## Downtime Activities

Between adventures, characters can engage in downtime. Common activities:

- **Crafting**: Requires proficiency with relevant tools. Progress = 5 gp of work per day. Cost = half the item's market price in raw materials.
- **Practicing a Profession**: Maintain a modest lifestyle at no cost.
- **Recuperating**: End one effect preventing HP recovery, or make a DC 15 CON save to end one disease/poison. Requires 3 days.
- **Researching**: Spend gold per day to get answers to specific questions (DM discretion on cost and time).
- **Training**: Learn a new language or tool proficiency. 250 days and 1 gp/day. Most tables reduce this significantly.

### Xanathar's Expanded Downtime (commonly used)
- **Carousing**: Make contacts. Roll on a social table.
- **Crime**: Make a check to pull a heist. Risk getting caught.
- **Gambling**: Contested checks. Win or lose gold.
- **Pit Fighting**: 3 checks (Athletics, Acrobatics, and a special ability). Win gold based on results.
- **Buying/Selling Magic Items**: Requires time and Investigation/Persuasion checks. Finding a buyer takes work.
- **Scribing Scrolls**: Time and cost by spell level. Requires the spell and proficiency with Arcana.
