# Tasha's Cauldron of Everything Subclasses

Expanded subclass options from TCoE. Each subclass is covered mechanically with features, scaling, and action economy. These are official expansions to the class system.

---

## Barbarian

### Path of the Beast

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Primal Path)

**Form of the Beast** (Level 3): Bonus action to activate. Rage and this feature share the same resource pool. When you activate, your unarmed strikes scale to d8 (or d10 if you have paws/claws, d12 if two-handed). Choose one manifestation:
- **Bite**: Unarmed strike deals 1d8 piercing (or your die size). On hit, target makes STR save (DC 8 + proficiency + STR mod) or is knocked prone. Costs a bonus action after hitting.
- **Claws**: Two unarmed strikes as a bonus action. Each is 1d6 slashing (or your die size). These replace your normal bonus action attack from raging.
- **Tail**: Reach increases by 5 ft for unarmed strikes. When a creature you can reach hits you, reaction to make one unarmed tail strike (1d6).

Feature scales: die size increases to d10 at level 11, d12 at level 17 (matching martial arts).

Practical note: Transforms barbarian into a beast form caster without full transformation; aggressive action economy play.

---

### Path of Wild Magic

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Primal Path)

**Wild Magic Surge** (Level 3): When you hit with a melee weapon during rage, roll d8. On 8, roll d6 and trigger a magical effect:
1. Teleport 30 ft to an unoccupied space you see. Same applies to a creature within 5 ft (target can resist and waste the effect).
2. Advantage on attack rolls until end of turn.
3.1 creature you see within 60 ft takes 1d6 force damage.
4. Heal yourself 1d6 HP (max 10 per rage).
5. Invisibility until end of next turn (or you attack/cast spell).
6. Next save you make before next turn is with advantage.

Roll during rage, not before. Keeps combats chaotic.

**Magical Awareness** (Level 6): You sense magic within 1 mile (60 ft indoors). When spell is cast within range, you know direction and strength (1st+ level spells only). Not a location, just awareness.

**Unstable Backlash** (Level 10): When you take damage during rage, reaction to trigger a wild magic surge (roll d6 immediately, no wait for hit).

**Controlled Surge** (Level 14): When you roll 8 on wild magic surge d8, you choose which d6 effect triggers instead of rolling.

Practical note: Unpredictable damage scaling and control gain; passive magical detection.

## Bard

### College of Creation

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Bard College)

**Mote of Inspiration** (Level 3): Your Bardic Inspiration die rolls are made by recipients when they use the inspiration, not when you grant it. Timing shift to action economy: grant inspiration, recipient uses later (within 10 minutes as normal).

**Performance of Creation** (Level 3): 10-minute ritual during short/long rest. Create one nonmagical object (cloth, rope, wood, stone, iron, etc.) of Tiny size. Max size scaling: Small at level 5, Medium at level 10. Objects last 1 hour (permanent if nonmetal, nonmagical). Gp limit by level: 10 gp at 3rd, 50 gp at 5th, 250 gp at 10th. Up to 4 objects at once.

Practical mechanic: Create mundane tools, lockpicks, rope, rope ladders, firewood. Not weapons or armor.

**Animating Performance** (Level 6): When you finish a short/long rest, you can animate one Medium or smaller nonmagical object or creature (corpse). It obeys simple commands, has AC 10, 1 HP, proficiency in STR save, can move and take actions on your turn (part of your action economy). Lasts 1 hour or until dispelled. Only one active at a time. Can reanimate objects up to 4 at once if they're from Performance of Creation. This is not True Polymorph or full control — it's a literal animated construct.

**Magical Secrets Expansion** (Level 14): You learn an additional spell from any class list beyond Magical Secrets choices.

Practical note: Control oriented, object/NPC manipulation via ritual casting and animated constructs.

---

### College of Eloquence

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Bard College)

**Silver Tongue** (Level 3): When you make a Persuasion check or Deception check, treat any roll of 9 or lower on the d20 as a 10. Minimum floor effect (like Rogue's Reliable Talent but just for two skills).

**Unsettling Words** (Level 3): When you hit a creature with a Bardic Inspiration die, use a reaction to expend one of your Bardic Inspiration dice and subtract the roll from that creature's next saving throw within 1 minute. Debuff applied after the inspiration is spent on a hit, so save is next save they make (attack rolls don't count, spells only). Costs a reaction + inspiration die.

**Unfailing Enchantment** (Level 6): When a creature you see within 60 ft is about to make a saving throw against a spell or ability you control (your spell, your subclass ability), you can use your reaction to grant that creature disadvantage on the save. Once per short rest.

Debuff to enemy saves without Heightened Spell — powerful action economy tool.

**Unaffected by Illusions** (Level 14): You have advantage on saves against illusion spells and effects that would charm or frighten you. Creatures charmed by you have disadvantage on saves if you use Unsettling Words in the same round.

Practical note: Control and persuasion focused; manipulation of saves and speech-based rolls.

## Cleric

### Order Domain

**Subclass at**: Level 1 (Divine Domain)

**Bonus Proficiencies** (Level 1): Medium armor, heavy armor, martial weapons.

**Order's Demand** (Level 1): Channel Divinity. Target creature within 30 ft makes WIS save or is charmed by you until end of your next turn. While charmed, each time the creature takes damage, it has disadvantage on the first saving throw it makes before the end of its next turn. No concentration — lasts the full duration or until you use it again. Movement restriction: charmed creature must move closest to you if possible on its turn.

Practical mechanic: Use this during combat when you want a single target to fail a save (action economy debuff).

**Voice of Authority** (Level 6): When another creature you can see within 30 ft damages a target, you can use your reaction to give that target advantage on the next saving throw it makes before the end of your next turn (if it's made within 1 minute). Conditions: only if you can see both the attacker and target. Action economy: costs a reaction, spreads saves around.

**Divine Strike** (Level 8): Once per turn when you hit a melee weapon attack, add 1d8 force damage (+1d8 at 14th level). Synergy with Extra Attack from multiclass or if you also get a bonus action strike.

**Order's Wrath** (Level 17): When a creature within 5 ft of you casts a spell or makes an attack roll, you can use your reaction to force a WIS save (DC 8 + proficiency + WIS mod). On fail, the creature takes 2d8 force damage and has disadvantage on the roll. On success, half damage, no disadvantage. Once per short rest.

Practical note: Control and damage mitigation; best with Extra Attack.

---

### Peace Domain

**Subclass at**: Level 1 (Divine Domain)

**Emboldening Bond** (Level 1): 10-minute ritual. Create mystical bonds with up to your proficiency bonus creatures within 30 ft (usually 5-6 creatures by level 5). Each bonded creature gains temporary HP = your WIS mod + half cleric level (rounded down). Once per short rest. When a bonded creature within 30 ft takes damage, you can use a reaction to give another bonded creature within 30 ft advantage on the next saving throw or attack roll before the end of its next turn. If damage taken reduces a creature to 0 HP, that creature gets temporary HP back (same calculation). **This is why Peace Domain gets banned at many tables.**

Practical mechanic: 1 reaction = advantage to another creature's roll. Stack this with other advantage sources, and with enough bonded creatures active, you can trigger the damage reaction constantly.

**Protective Bond** (Level 6): When a bonded creature within 30 ft is hit by an attack roll, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on that roll if you can see the attacker. No save, no action economy cost beyond the reaction. Once per round.

**Divine Strike** (Level 8): Once per turn when you hit a melee weapon attack, add 1d8 psychic damage (+1d8 at 14th).

**Potent Spellcasting** (Level 17): Add your WIS mod to damage rolls of cleric spells.

Practical note: Stack with Twilight for unlimited temporary HP and advantage spreads. Banned in AL and many organized play.

---

### Twilight Domain

**Subclass at**: Level 1 (Divine Domain)

**Twilight Sanctuary** (Level 1): Channel Divinity. Choose a point you can see within 30 ft. Sphere of dim light (30 ft radius) emanates from that point for 1 minute. Creatures of your choice within the sphere gain temporary HP = 1d6 + cleric level (at level 2, that's 1d6+2; at level 17, 1d6+17 — stacking free HP). When a bonded creature within the sphere finishes a short or long rest, it gains those temporary HP when rest ends. Creatures can't be frightened while in the sphere. **Broken mechanic: free large-scale temporary HP that stacks with Peace Domain's bonus action advantage reactions.**

**Channel Divinity: Twilight Sanctuary** triggers every short rest if the table is long and multiple shorts rest happen. Each rest grants new temp HP.

**Divine Strike** (Level 8): Once per turn when you hit a melee weapon attack, add 1d8 radiant damage (+1d8 at 14th).

**Twilight Mastery** (Level 17): When you cast a spell that affects vision, range, or sight (Darkness, Moonbeam, etc.), creatures within 30 ft of you gain the benefits instead (if they're allies). This spreads effect range dramatically.

Practical note: Free large-scale temporary HP and fear immunity stacking. Strong and often restricted.

## Druid

### Circle of Spores

**Subclass at**: Level 2 (Druid Circle)

**Fungal Infestation** (Level 2): When a creature within 10 ft of you dies, you can use a reaction to roll 2d8 + druid level spores. That many temporary HP manifest for you. When you hit a creature with a melee attack, you can deal extra 1d8 poison damage (costs 1 spore charge, refreshes on kill or short rest).

Practical mechanic: Each kill resets the spore pool. Spores = temporary combat resource, not full turn economy.

**Symbiotic Entity** (Level 2): Bonus action to activate. You take on fungal symbiosis: gain temporary HP equal to druid level + WIS mod. AC becomes 10 + DEX mod + WIS mod (capped at your regular AC). Unarmed strikes deal 1d8 + WIS mod damage (or 1d6 + WIS if Small or Tiny). Lasts 10 minutes. Uses = proficiency bonus per long rest. While active, you can use a bonus action to give yourself the symbiotic entity temporary HP (same calculation) without expending a use.

**Spore Cloud** (Level 6): When Symbiotic Entity is active, reaction to reduce damage you take by half (and affected creature takes 2d8 poison damage, no save). Damage reduction + AoE damage combo.

**Fungal Body** (Level 10): You take no damage from nonmagical weapons while Symbiotic Entity is active. Magic weapons and spells still hit.

**Infinite Spores** (Level 14): Symbiotic Entity no longer requires uses per long rest — activate as often as you want (still requires bonus action).

Practical note: Damage reduction tank with poison scaling; hybrid melee + spell casting.

---

### Circle of Stars

**Subclass at**: Level 2 (Druid Circle)

**Star Map** (Level 2): When you finish a long rest, you can choose one of three constellations to attune to for 8 hours (ending at next long rest).
- **Archer**: Ranged attack rolls within 30 ft gain advantage. Costs no action or bonus action — passive.
- **Chalice**: When you cast a healing spell, targets gain temporary HP = spell level (free addition, no action economy cost).
- **Dragon**: When you make a STR save, you can use a reaction to gain advantage on that save. Once per short rest.

**Starry Form** (Level 2): Bonus action to assume stellar form. Your unarmed strike damage becomes 1d8 + WIS mod (or 1d6 if Small). AC becomes 10 + DEX mod + WIS mod. Lasts 10 minutes. Uses = proficiency bonus per long rest. Hover speed = 30 ft. You can cast spells while in form.

Practical mechanic: Starry Form is a separate AC/damage boost from Wild Shape. Uses = wildshape-like resource.

**Cosmic Mind** (Level 6): Once per turn within Starry Form, a creature within 30 ft that you can see must make WIS save or take 1d8 + WIS mod psychic damage (no save on attack roll — automatic psychic rider).

**Full of Stars** (Level 10): While in Starry Form, you shed dim light in 15 ft radius. Creatures that start their turn in the light gain 1 temporary HP + are cured of exhaustion once in the light.

**Twinkling Constellations** (Level 14): You can attune to two constellations at once (still choosing from Archer, Chalice, Dragon). Activate both bonuses simultaneously.

Practical note: Hybrid form with spell casting + constellation passive bonuses; AoE healing via Chalice.

---

### Circle of Wildfire

**Subclass at**: Level 2 (Druid Circle)

**Wildfire Spirit** (Level 2): As an action, you summon a fire elemental spirit in an unoccupied space within 30 ft that you can see. It manifests as a Tiny flaming creature (flavor, not mechanically distinct). Spirit lasts 1 hour or until dismissed. Uses = proficiency bonus per long rest.

Spirit stats: AC 13, 5 + 5 per druid level (at level 2, 15 HP; at level 17, 90 HP). Speed 30 ft.

Actions: **Flame Strike** — melee attack, 1d6 + WIS mod fire damage. You can use your bonus action to command it to move up to 30 ft and attack.

**Wildfire Bond** (Level 2): When the spirit hits with an attack, target takes an additional 1d6 fire damage. At higher levels, scales to 2d6 at 6th, 3d6 at 10th. When you cast a spell that deals damage within 30 ft of the spirit, each creature in a 5 ft radius centered on the spirit takes that damage type +1d6 (no save, automatic rider).

Practical mechanic: Spirit grants an extra action economy turn, and spells gain built-in AoE via spirit proximity.

**Blazing Revitalization** (Level 6): When you cast a spell with a fire damage rider near the spirit, that spell's targets also heal 1d6 HP (spirit-centric healing incentive).

**Fiery Teleportation** (Level 10): When the spirit uses Flame Strike and hits, it can teleport up to 15 ft to an unoccupied space you can see (no action cost). This enables kiting and repositioning.

**Blazing Reinforce** (Level 14): When the spirit is summoned or healed to full (via reinvoking during a rest), it gains 2d6 temporary HP.

Practical note: Summoned ally with bonus action economy; AoE spell amplification via proximity; reactive healing.

## Fighter

### Psi Warrior

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Martial Archetype)

**Psionic Power** (Level 3): You gain a pool of Psionic Energy points = fighter level. Recharges on short rest. Fuel your following reactions:

**Psionic Strike** (Level 3): When you hit with a weapon attack, spend 1-5 psionic points. Deal an extra 1d6 psychic damage per point spent (max 5d6). Does not require bonus action — rider to attack.

**Protective Field** (Level 3): Reaction when you or ally within 30 ft takes damage, spend 1-5 points, reduce damage by 1d6 per point (max 5d6). Once per round.

**Psionic Teleportation** (Level 3): Bonus action, spend 1-5 points, teleport up to 5 ft per point (max 25 ft). You can bring one willing creature you're grappling. No sight required.

**Telekinetic Movement** (Level 7): Reaction to bonus action to move a creature or object within 30 ft that you can see up to 5 ft per point spent (1-5 points). Objects max 5 pounds per point. Creatures make STR save; on fail, they move as directed.

**Guarded Mind** (Level 10): Advantage on saves against spells and psychic damage. Reaction to an enemy within 30 ft casting a spell, spend 2 points to give them disadvantage on the attack or save DC (once per spell cast).

**Bulwark of Force** (Level 18): When Protective Field reduces damage to 0 HP or below zero, the creature gains temporary HP = amount blocked beyond zero.

Practical note: Resource-hungry reactions that spread psionic points thin; mobile tank with teleport.

### Rune Knight

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Martial Archetype)

**Runecraft** (Level 3): When you finish a long rest, you can inscribe magical runes on one melee weapon you're proficient with or on armor you're wearing. Choose two runes from the list below. Each rune has a bonus action activation during combat.

**Rune Activation** (Level 3): Uses = fighter level / 2 (rounded down). Recharge on short rest.

Rune options (choose 2 at level 3, gain more at 7, 10, 15):

1. **Frost Rune**: Bonus action to activate on a creature within 30 ft you can see. Target makes STR save. On fail, movement speed is halved, attacks with disadvantage until end of next turn. On success, no effect.

2. **Stone Rune**: Bonus action. Creatures within 30 ft moving toward you must make WIS save or their movement speed is 0 until end of current turn (they're frozen in place). Once triggered per activation.

3. **Fire Rune**: Bonus action. Target within 30 ft makes DEX save. On fail, 2d6 + fighter level fire damage; on success, half. Increases to 3d6 at level 7, 4d6 at level 15.

4. **Tempest Rune**: Bonus action. You teleport up to 30 ft to an unoccupied space you can see. Each creature within 5 ft of your departure or arrival makes STR save or takes 1d6 + STR mod damage. Increases to 2d6 at level 7, 3d6 at level 15.

5. **Cloud Rune**: Bonus action on hit. Instead of dealing extra damage, you teleport the hit creature up to 5 ft (you choose direction). If it impacts a solid object, it takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage per 5 ft traveled.

6. **Giant Rune**: Bonus action on hit. Target becomes frightened of you until end of next turn (WIS save negates). Advantage on checks against frightened creature.

**Giant's Might** (Level 3): When you use Action Surge, you become Huge size for 1 minute (or until you dismiss it). Reach increases by 5 ft. Weapon damage increases by 1d6. You gain advantage on STR checks and saves. Once per short rest (not once per Action Surge activation — once per combat round type).

Practical mechanic: Separate resource from Action Surge; gain reach and damage scaling temporarily.

**Draconic Resilience** (Level 7): Add proficiency bonus to initiative rolls. Gain resistance to one damage type you choose when you inscribe runes (scales with short rest).

**Runic Shield** (Level 10): When a creature within 30 ft that you can see deals damage to you or an ally, reaction to spend a Rune Activation use to gain resistance to that damage (applies to all damage from that source until end of turn). Half damage taken.

**Great Runic Power** (Level 15): You can inscribe three runes instead of two. Gain an additional Rune Activation use.

**Master of Runes** (Level 18): Rune Activation uses reset on long rest only, but you can perform one rune activation per turn as a bonus action without spending a use, once per short rest. This is a free rune activation per short rest.

Practical note: Versatile control runes with bonus action economy; Giant's Might for temporary reach/damage/advantage.

## Monk

### Way of Mercy

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Monastic Tradition)

**Monastic Tradition: Mercy** (Level 3): When you finish a long rest, you gain 4 ki points reserved for Hands of Healing and Hands of Harm (separate from normal Ki pool). These don't count against maximum Ki.

**Hands of Healing** (Level 3): When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, spend 1 mercy ki point to restore 1d4 + WIS mod + monk level HP to that creature (or any creature you choose within 5 ft of the hit). No limit to healing per turn, just consume ki points.

Practical mechanic: Unarmed strike becomes healing vector; action economy: strike as action, healing rider.

**Hands of Harm** (Level 3): When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, spend 1 mercy ki point to deal extra 1d4 poison damage (added to attack). Targets don't get to save — poison damage applies on hit.

**Physician's Touch** (Level 6): When you spend ki for healing, multiply the healing by your proficiency bonus. Healing = (1d4 + WIS mod + monk level) × proficiency. This skyrockets at higher levels (×4 at level 17).

**Hand of Eventual Harm** (Level 11): When a creature you hit with Hands of Harm within the last minute takes damage from any source (including you), you can use a reaction to deal additional poison damage = 1d4 + WIS mod + monk level (free reaction, no ki cost). Poison damage delayed until next damage event.

**Hand of the Healing Master** (Level 17): When you cast a healing spell or use Hands of Healing, affected creature gains temporary HP = monk level.

Practical note: Dual healing and poison damage via unarmed strikes; conditional reaction healing triggers.

---

### Way of the Astral Self

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Monastic Tradition)

**Arms of the Astral Self** (Level 3): As a bonus action, you summon spectral arms attached to you (flavor; not a separate creature). Duration = 10 minutes. Uses = proficiency bonus per long rest.

While active, you can make unarmed strikes using WIS instead of STR/DEX (your choice each attack). Unarmed strike damage becomes 1d8 + WIS mod (instead of d4 + DEX mod). Reach extends by 5 ft for your unarmed strikes. You make two unarmed strikes as part of the Attack action (bonus to attack economy vs normal flurry).

**Astral Awareness** (Level 3): When Arms are active, you have advantage on Initiative checks. You can sense the location of creatures within 10 ft even if you can't see them (blind sense, no action).

**Visage of the Astral Self** (Level 6): While Arms are active, you can use a bonus action to blind a creature within 10 ft that you can see. Target makes CON save (DC 8 + proficiency + WIS mod). On fail, it can't see until the end of its next turn. Each creature only once per Arms activation.

**One with the Astral Self** (Level 11): When Arms are active, your unarmed strike damage increases to 1d10 + WIS mod. AC increases by your WIS mod (capped at +5) while arms are active.

**Awakened Astral Self** (Level 17): When you activate Arms as a bonus action, you can immediately make one unarmed strike as part of the bonus action (no action economy cost — summon + strike in one bonus action).

Practical note: WIS-based unarmed strikes with reach and bonus mobility; spiritual form combat.

## Paladin

### Oath of Glory

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Sacred Oath)

**Tenets of Glory**: Pursue physical excellence and victory.

**Channel Divinity Options**:

1. **Peerless Athlete** (Level 3): Channel Divinity (action). For 10 minutes, you gain advantage on STR and DEX checks, gain +5 ft movement speed, can jump twice as far. Once per short rest (standard Channel Divinity slot).

2. **Avenging Strike** (Level 3): When you hit a melee weapon attack, spend a Channel Divinity to trigger a secondary effect. Choose one:
   - The hit creature takes additional 2d8 + paladin level force damage.
   - You gain advantage on your next attack roll before the end of your next turn.
   - An ally within 30 ft gains advantage on next attack before end of turn.

Oath spell list always prepared:
- Level 3: Guiding Bolt, Heroism
- Level 5: Enhance Ability, Magic Weapon
- Level 9: Haste, Hypnotic Pattern
- Level 13: Dominate Person, Stoneskin
- Level 17: Telekinesis, Animate Objects

**Extra Attack** (Level 5): Two attacks per Attack action (standard paladin feature).

**Glorious Defense** (Level 7): When you hit a melee weapon attack, you can choose to reduce damage by your CHA modifier (minimum 1) instead of getting the attack damage bonus from Divine Smite. This is a rider to attack, chosen after hit:

Practical mechanic: Each attack can be offensive (Smite) or defensive (reduce damage). Choose post-hit.

**Living Legend** (Level 15): When you use Avenging Strike or Peerless Athlete, allies within 30 ft who can see you gain temporary HP = CHA modifier. You regain 1 Channel Divinity use at the start of your turn if you have no uses left.

Practical note: Mobile offensive paladin with movement/athletics scaling; Channel Divinity refresh mechanic.

---

### Oath of the Watchers

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Sacred Oath)

**Tenets of Watchers**: Protect the Material Plane from otherworldly threats.

**Channel Divinity Options**:

1. **Watcher's Will** (Level 3): Channel Divinity (reaction when you make a save). You gain advantage on the save. Once per short rest.

2. **Abjure Enemy** (Level 3): Channel Divinity (action). Target creature within 30 ft makes CHA save (DC 8 + proficiency + CHA). On fail, it takes 2d8 + CHA mod radiant damage and is pushed 10 ft away. On success, no damage, no push. Once per short rest.

Oath spell list always prepared:
- Level 3: Alarm, Detect Magic
- Level 5: Detect Thoughts, Phantasmal Force
- Level 9: Dispel Magic, Nondetection
- Level 13: Arcane Eye, Banishment
- Level 17: Commune with Deity, Legend Lore

**Truesight** (Level 7): You gain truesight to 120 ft (see through darkness, illusions, invisible creatures, shapes). No longer limited by magical disguise/invisibility vs you. Constant, no action.

**Piercing Sight** (Level 7): When you finish a long rest, you can attune to one willing creature within 30 ft. When that creature attacks a target or makes a save, you can use a reaction to grant them advantage on the roll. Once per short rest.

**Warding Flare** (Level 10): Reaction when an attack roll is made against you or ally within 30 ft (you can see). Impose disadvantage on the roll. Once per short rest (recharges but limited).

**Unrelenting Sentinel** (Level 15): When a creature within 30 ft misses a save against your spell or ability, you can use a reaction to force it to repeat the save (same DC). Can repeat once per creature per long rest.

Practical note: Reliable save-based paladin with constant truesight; ally support via advantage spreading.

## Ranger

### Fey Wanderer

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Ranger Archetype)

**Wanderer's Magics** (Level 3): You learn spells even outside ranger spells. Choose one spell from bard, cleric, druid, or ranger list (Tasha's optional features give you a fey spellcasting focus).

**Dreadful Strikes** (Level 3): When you hit a melee weapon attack, add 1d4 + ranger level psychic damage. This increases to 2d4 + ranger level at level 11. Does not require Ki or resources — always triggers on hit.

**Fey Reinforcements** (Level 3): When you take a short rest, you can summon 2d4 + ranger level small fey creatures (sprites, pixies, etc.) within 30 ft. They act on your initiative. AC 10, 1 HP each, can move and take actions on your turn. Use = proficiency bonus per long rest. Lasts 1 hour.

Practical mechanic: Action economy multiplier; each creature takes a turn, but they use your action pool.

**Fey Wanderer's Cunning** (Level 6): Advantage on WIS (Insight) checks. When you hit with Dreadful Strikes, subtract up to your WIS modifier from the psychic damage roll and add that amount to an ally's AC until end of next turn (redistribute damage to AC).

**Fey Reinforcement Improvement** (Level 11): Your summoned fey gain +2 AC and resistance to one damage type (you choose at rest). They can make opportunity attacks on your turn (reaction economy).

**Dreadful Strikes Scaling** (Level 11): Dreadful Strikes deal 2d4 + ranger level psychic damage (doubled from level 3 version).

**Fey Resilience** (Level 15): You and creatures summoned by Fey Reinforcements gain resistance to psychic damage and condition immunity to exhaustion while within 30 ft of each other.

Practical note: Summon-focused ranger with ally-syncing damage; condition immunity.

---

### Swarmkeeper

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Ranger Archetype)

**Swarm** (Level 3): You gain a magical swarm (wasps, locusts, spiders, etc.; flavor doesn't matter mechanically). The swarm occupies your space and moves with you. It's not a separate creature.

**Swarmkeeper's Strikes** (Level 3): When you hit a melee weapon attack, you can choose to deal extra damage. Instead of rolling damage, spend 1-5 ranger ki (if you have ki from multiclass) or just add up to 1d6 per ranger proficiency bonus (choose 1d6 to 1d6×proficiency, max). This rider damage is force or poison (you choose per attack, no save).

Practical mechanic: Each attack, you can spend into extra damage or leave it standard. Scales with proficiency.

**Swarm Cloud** (Level 3): Bonus action, the swarm creates a 15 ft aura of difficult terrain centered on you until end of turn. Creatures that start their turn in the aura take 1d6 poison damage (your choice of creatures get a DEX save for half). Once per short rest, but can be repeated if you burn action economy.

**Swarm Distraction** (Level 6): When a creature attacks you or an ally within 5 ft, you can use a reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. No save. Once per short rest.

**Swarmkeeper's Evasion** (Level 11): When you take DEX damage, you can use a reaction to reduce damage by 1d6 + ranger level. Once per short rest.

**Swarm Expansion** (Level 15): Swarm Cloud radius increases to 30 ft. Damage increases to 2d6 + ranger level poison.

Practical note: Passive terrain hazard with reactive defense; damage rider scaling.

## Rogue

### Phantom

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Roguish Archetype)

**Whispers of the Dead** (Level 3): When you finish a short or long rest, you can communicate with one humanoid spirit within 30 ft (typically, a dead person). Gain information about events the spirit witnessed while alive and within 30 ft. No mechanical combat benefit — flavor + information.

**Tokens of the Departed** (Level 3): When a humanoid dies within 30 ft of you, you can use a reaction to capture part of its essence in a small object (coin, ring, etc.). You can spend 10 minutes in meditation to speak with this token and gain advantage on one ability check related to that creature's knowledge (history, religion about that creature, etc.). You can hold up to proficiency bonus tokens.

**Wails from the Grave** (Level 3): When you finish a short rest, you can spend 1-3 Rogue points to gain that many ghostly allies (spectral forms). When you hit with Sneak Attack, each ghost that's active deals 1d6 force damage to the same target (no save). Ghosts last 1 hour. Each ghost = 1 Rogue point per turn you maintain them (pay at start of turn or they fade).

Practical mechanic: Sneak Attack scaling via spending points for ghost multipliers; action economy cost.

**Tokens of Loyalty** (Level 6): When you capture a token, you gain a ghostly form that mimics the departed. You can cast spells as if you were that creature (if it was a spellcaster). Costs 2 Rogue points per spell slot level expended.

**Ghost Form** (Level 9): Bonus action, spend 3 Rogue points to become invisible and ethereal for 10 minutes. You have a fly speed of 30 ft and can move through walls/objects. Attacks against you have disadvantage. You can end the form early or be forced out by Turn Undead or similar effects.

**Death Knell** (Level 13): When a creature within 30 ft of you dies, you can use a reaction to gain temporary HP = creature's CR + your proficiency bonus. You also gain 1 additional Rogue point.

**Whispers Master** (Level 17): When you spend Rogue points on Wails from the Grave, each ghost deals 2d6 force damage instead of 1d6.

Practical note: Rogue that scales via spending points on ghostly multipliers and temporary HP; invisible assassin option via Ghost Form.

### Soulknife

**Subclass at**: Level 3 (Roguish Archetype)

**Psychic Blades** (Level 3): You can use a bonus action to manifest a psychic blade in your hand. It's a finesse melee weapon (1d4 damage). When you hit with it, you add 1d6 psychic damage (scales to 2d6 at level 9, 3d6 at level 13, 4d6 at level 17). Weapon disappears when you let go.

You can have two blades active (dual wield with DEX). Attack rolls use DEX + proficiency. Sneak Attack applies to Psychic Blades.

**Psychic Armor** (Level 3): When you manifest a blade, you gain psychic armor (no armor worn). AC = 10 + DEX mod + proficiency (similar to Unarmored Defense but includes proficiency). Lasts 1 hour or until all blades dismissed.

**Soul Blades of Resistance** (Level 6): When you hit with a Psychic Blade, you can choose to reduce the psychic damage by your proficiency bonus and grant resistance to that damage type to an ally within 30 ft until end of next turn (distribute psychic damage to defense).

**Psychic Teleportation** (Level 9): When you hit with a Psychic Blade, you can spend Rogue points equal to the damage dealt (e.g., if you dealt 8 psychic damage, 8 points) to teleport up to 30 ft to an unoccupied space you can see. This incentivizes high-damage rolls.

**Veil of Shadows** (Level 13): You can spend 5 Rogue points to become invisible until end of your next turn (or you attack). Resets if you attack or cast a spell.

**Master of Blades** (Level 17): Psychic Blades deal additional damage equal to twice your proficiency bonus (not doubled psychic die, just flat bonus). You can manifest up to three blades at once (three-weapon wielding with one as bonus action attack).

Practical note: DEX-based psychic damage with scaling and teleport option; invisibility refresh.

## Sorcerer

### Aberrant Mind

**Subclass at**: Level 1 (Sorcerous Origin)

**Psionic Sorcery** (Level 1): You learn all sorcerer spells plus additional spells from the Aberrant Mind list (bard, cleric, warlock spells). Spellcasting becomes psionic — you can cast spells without verbal or somatic components (reading from your mind only). You can cast spells even while bound, gagged, or in situations where you can't speak/move hands.

Practical mechanic: Subtle Spell without using sorcery points; can cast in silence.

**Telepathic Speech** (Level 1): You can spend 1 sorcery point to cast any spell you know as a bonus action instead of an action. Additional cost: the spell must be 3rd level or lower, or you must use two sorcery points. This is Quickened Spell + Subtle Spell combined cost-wise.

**Revelation in Flesh** (Level 6): When you gain a Sorcery Point, you can manifest one psionic ability:
- **Psionic Resilience**: Gain resistance to one damage type until end of turn.
- **Psionic Leap**: You can teleport up to 30 ft to an unoccupied space you can see.
- **Psionic Mind Swap**: Swap initiative with one creature within 30 ft (advantage to you next turn, disadvantage to them).

No action required — manifests automatically when you gain points.

**Psychic Defenses** (Level 6): Advantage on saves against spells and telepathic effects. When a creature within 30 ft deals psychic damage to you, you can use a reaction to subtract 1d6 + sorcerer level from that damage.

**Revelation in Flesh: Physical Manifestation** (Level 14): When you take Revelation, also gain temporary HP = sorcerer level.

**Revelation in Flesh: Unlimited Manifestation** (Level 18): When you finish a short rest, gain 2 automatic manifestations per turn (no sorcery point cost).

Practical note: Always-on subtle spellcasting; bonus action casting for lower-level spells; passive psionic reactivity.

### Clockwork Soul

**Subclass at**: Level 1 (Sorcerous Origin)

**Clockwork Magic** (Level 1): Your sorcerer spellcasting is infused with order magic. You gain the Mending cantrip. You can learn spells from cleric or wizard lists. Your spellcasting learns one bonus spell each time you can learn spells (in addition to normal spell learning).

**Restore Balance** (Level 1): When a creature you can see within 60 ft makes a saving throw or ability check, you can spend 2 sorcery points as a reaction to impose advantage (if you want them to succeed) or disadvantage (if you want them to fail) on that roll. This applies to any roll, not just the spell it triggers.

Practical mechanic: Spend 2 points = flip coin on any save/check in 60 ft range. Stacks with Heightened Spell or Careful Spell effects.

**Bastion of Law** (Level 6): When you cast a spell that targets allies or yourself, affected creatures gain temporary HP = spell level (free addition, no action economy cost). Temporary HP can stack.

**Clockwork Regression** (Level 6): When you hit a creature with a sorcerer spell (damage or control), you can spend 1 sorcery point to force it to repeat the save at disadvantage (cost per spell cast, once per spell).

**Gears of Order** (Level 14): When you use Restore Balance to affect a save/check, you can affect up to two creatures instead of one (same 2 point cost for both). They can be affected by advantage or disadvantage independently.

**Perfect Calculation** (Level 18): When you finish a long rest, gain 5 temporary sorcery points that don't count against your maximum. When you use Restore Balance, those temporary points are consumed first before regular points.

Practical note: Control save/check outcome with resource spend; stacking temporary HP; guaranteed points per rest.

## Warlock

### The Fathomless

**Subclass at**: Level 1 (Otherworldly Patron)

**Tentacle of the Deep** (Level 1): You can create a spectral tentacle within 30 ft as a bonus action (lasts 1 minute). It has 30 ft reach. When a creature within reach attacks you or an ally, you can use a reaction to have the tentacle deal 1d8 + CHA mod psychic damage to that creature (no save). Once per reaction per tentacle summoning.

Recharge: When you finish a short rest, you can summon the tentacle again.

**Gift of the Sea** (Level 1): You gain a swim speed of 30 ft and can breathe underwater. You gain advantage on saves against being restrained or grappled.

**Slippery Mind** (Level 6): When you make a saving throw, you can add your CHA mod to that roll (before rolling, committing) once per short rest.

**Fathomless Reach** (Level 6): Your Tentacle of the Deep reach extends to 60 ft (doubled). When it hits with damage, a creature takes additional 1d6 psychic damage (stacking with base tentacle damage).

**Guardian Tentacle** (Level 10): When a creature within 30 ft takes damage, you can use a reaction to have your tentacle grapple that creature (no save; it's grappled until end of your next turn, if tentacle is active). Can grapple up to proficiency bonus creatures at once.

**Fathomless Master** (Level 14): Your tentacle damage increases to 2d8 + CHA mod. You can maintain multiple tentacles simultaneously (one per short rest recharge, stacking).

Practical note: Reactive tentacle attacks with scaling reach and psychic rider damage; underwater adaptation.

### The Genie

**Subclass at**: Level 1 (Otherworldly Patron)

**Genie's Vessel** (Level 1): Your patron grants you a magical vessel (lamp, ring, stone, etc.). It's an object you can use as a spellcasting focus. When you cast a spell using Pact Magic, you can gain temporary HP = spell level (free addition, no action economy). Once per short rest, you can spend 1 warlock spell slot to regain that many temporary HP.

**Genie's Limit** (Level 1): You and your vessel are linked. If the vessel is destroyed, you can recreate it during a short rest (no cost). The vessel cannot be stolen (magically attuned to you).

**Expanded Spellcasting** (Level 1): Your warlock spell list expands with Genie-specific spells. You learn one bonus spell each Warlock level (instead of normal spell progression).

**Genie's Wrath** (Level 1): When you cast a spell that deals damage, add CHA mod to the damage (if it's not already adding CHA). Applies to Eldritch Blast, Scorching Ray, Fireball, etc.

Practical mechanic: Automatic damage scaling to all damage spells; bonus temporary HP on casting.

**Elemental Gift** (Level 6): Choose an elemental affinity at level 1: fire, cold, lightning, thunder. Your damage spells deal that damage type. When you deal that damage type, the target must make a save or be affected by a condition (fire = restrained by flames, cold = slowed, lightning = stunned until end of next turn, thunder = knocked prone).

No save if damage = 0 (immunity). Save DC = 8 + proficiency + CHA mod.

**Sanctuary Vessel** (Level 10): As an action, you can use your vessel as a refuge. You and up to 5 creatures within 10 ft of the vessel enter it (extradimensional space, Large size). You can see outside but not vice versa. Lasts 8 hours or until you dismiss. Lasts 1 hour per short rest remaining.

Creatures inside gain resistance to the chosen elemental damage type.

**Elemental Companion** (Level 14): When you finish a short rest, you can summon an elemental (tiny flame, ice cube, air sprite, or stone golem, matching your elemental choice). It acts on your initiative, has AC 12, 10 + CHA mod HP. Can move and act on your turn. Lasts 1 hour.

**Master of Genies** (Level 18): Your Genie's Wrath bonus increases to 2 × CHA mod. Spell slots restore on short rest (standard warlock), but you gain one additional slot per short rest if you use your vessel as a spellcasting focus.

Practical note: Temporary HP generation with damage scaling; elemental theming with condition riders; extradimensional sanctuary.

## Wizard

### Bladesinging

**Subclass at**: Level 2 (Arcane Tradition)

**Bladesinger's Training** (Level 2): You gain proficiency with scimitars (or one martial melee weapon). You can use DEX instead of STR for melee attacks with that weapon, and you can use DEX as your spellcasting ability modifier for attack rolls with the weapon (if you choose).

Practical mechanic: One weapon = DEX + INT for damage and attacks. You can smite with spell slot bonus damage while using the weapon.

**Bladesong** (Level 2): Bonus action to activate. You gain:
- AC = 10 + DEX mod + INT mod (instead of 10 + DEX mod for most wizards, a significant boost).
- Movement speed +10 ft.
- Advantage on DEX saves.
- Weapon attacks deal extra 1d6 damage (cumulative with other bonuses).

Duration = 1 minute. Uses = proficiency bonus per long rest. You lose these benefits if you're knocked prone or wear armor/shield.

While active, you can cast a spell and make a weapon attack with your bonus action (not Flurry of Blows, just added action economy).

**Extra Attack** (Level 6): When you take the Attack action, you make two attacks with your Bladesinging weapon (not Extra Attack from Fighter, but similar scaling). Applies only while Bladesong is active.

**Song of Defense** (Level 6): When you take damage while Bladesong is active, you can use your reaction to reduce damage taken by 1d6 + INT mod. Costs 1 Bladesong activation per reaction (so you have limited uses, measured in short rests).

**Improved Critical** (Level 10): Your Bladesinging weapon attacks score critical hits on 19-20 (not just 20).

**Song of Victory** (Level 14): At the end of your turn while Bladesong is active, you regain hit points = 1 + INT mod (once per turn, if you hit during that turn with your weapon).

**Master Bladesinger** (Level 18): When you activate Bladesong, you gain temporary HP = wizard level + INT mod. Song of Defense no longer costs Bladesong activation uses (unlimited uses, just reaction).

Practical note: INT-scaling melee wizard with temporary HP and action economy bonus; AC scaling via INT.

### Order of Scribes

**Subclass at**: Level 2 (Arcane Tradition)

**Wizardly Quill** (Level 2): You gain a magical quill. When you finish a long rest, the quill can transcribe spells from scrolls to your spellbook. You can have the quill transcribe one spell as a bonus action per day (normally transcribing takes 2 hours). The quill doesn't require you to be present (order it to transcribe and it works overnight).

Practical mechanic: Accelerated spellbook learning; normal transcription still available at 2 hours per spell.

**Arcane Grimoire** (Level 2): Your spellbook is a grimoire with magical properties. When you cast a spell from the grimoire, you can add your INT mod to the damage roll (if the spell deals damage and isn't already adding INT). When you finish a short rest, your grimoire grants you temporary HP = INT mod + wizard level (resets each short rest).

**Manifest Mind** (Level 2): As an action, you can create a spectral manifestation of your mind (a small spirit form). It occupies a space within 60 ft. When you cast a spell, the mind can choose to add advantage to the spell's attack roll or saving throw DC (you choose, per spell cast). Lasts 10 minutes. Uses = proficiency bonus per long rest.

You can use the mind's location to determine spell range (spells cast from the mind's location instead of yours, mechanically extending spell range by 60 ft).

**One with the Word** (Level 6): When a creature within 30 ft of you or your Manifest Mind makes a save against a spell you cast, you can use a reaction to impose disadvantage on that save (costs a reaction, no action cost). Once per short rest.

**Mystical Runes** (Level 10): When you transcribe spells into your grimoire, you can place protective wards. When a creature other than you reads a warded spell, it takes 2d6 psychic damage (no save). Each ward can trigger once per long rest.

You can place wards = proficiency bonus.

**Infuse Grimoire** (Level 14): When you finish a short rest with your grimoire, choose one spell of 1st or 2nd level in it. You can cast that spell without expending a spell slot once before the next rest (free cast).

**Master Scribe** (Level 18): Your Manifest Mind can cast spells you know (using your spell slots). Spells cast from the mind's location ignore total cover, so spells can reach around walls. You can maintain two Manifest Minds simultaneously (one per activation, but you can activate twice per long rest).

Practical note: Grimoire-powered spellcasting with wards; range extension via Manifest Mind; temporary HP via grimoire.
