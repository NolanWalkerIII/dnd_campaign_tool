# Magic Items Reference

## Rarity Tiers

| Rarity | Typical Level Range | Value Range (DMG) |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| Common | 1+ | 50-100 gp |
| Uncommon | 1+ | 101-500 gp |
| Rare | 5+ | 501-5,000 gp |
| Very Rare | 11+ | 5,001-50,000 gp |
| Legendary | 17+ | 50,001+ gp |
| Artifact | Unique | Priceless |

These level ranges are suggestions, not hard rules. A level 3 party finding a rare item isn't game-breaking — it depends on the specific item.

## Attunement

Some items require attunement — a short rest spent focusing on the item. Rules:
- A creature can attune to a maximum of **3 items** at a time.
- Attunement ends if you're more than 100 ft from the item for 24 hours, you die, or another creature attunes to it.
- Some items have attunement prerequisites (e.g., "requires attunement by a cleric" or "requires attunement by a creature of evil alignment").
- You can voluntarily end attunement during a short rest.

The 3-item limit is a real constraint at higher levels. Choosing which items to attune to is a meaningful decision.

## Identifying Magic Items

- **Short rest**: Spend a short rest focusing on the item to learn its properties and how to use it.
- **Identify spell**: Reveals all properties instantly (but not curses in some interpretations).
- **Detect Magic**: Tells you something is magical and its school, but not specific properties.

## Weapons

### +X Weapons
- **+1 Weapon** (Uncommon): +1 to attack and damage rolls. The bread-and-butter magic weapon.
- **+2 Weapon** (Rare): +2 to attack and damage.
- **+3 Weapon** (Very Rare): +3 to attack and damage. Near endgame.

All +X weapons count as magical for overcoming resistance/immunity to nonmagical attacks.

### Notable Weapons (SRD)

**Flame Tongue** (Rare, attunement): Bonus action to ignite — deals extra 2d6 fire damage on hit. Sheds bright light 40 ft, dim 40 ft while lit. Strong on classes with Extra Attack.

**Frost Brand** (Very Rare, attunement): Extra 1d6 cold damage. Fire resistance. When drawn, shed bright light 10 ft, dim 10 ft, extinguish nonmagical flames within 30 ft.

**Giant Slayer** (Rare): +1 weapon. Against giants, +1d6 damage and the giant must make DC 15 STR save or fall prone.

**Dragon Slayer** (Rare): +1 weapon. +3d6 damage against dragons.

**Vorpal Sword** (Legendary, attunement): +3 to attack and damage. On a natural 20, cut off a head (if the creature has one and can die from losing it). If no head to cut, deal extra 6d8 slashing.

**Sun Blade** (Rare, attunement): Longsword stats, but deals radiant damage. +2 to attack and damage. Extra 1d8 radiant vs. undead. Sheds sunlight (bright 15 ft, dim 15 ft). Finesse property. One of the best rare weapons in the game.

**Luck Blade** (Legendary, attunement): +1 sword. +1 to saving throws while held. 1d4-1 charges of Wish. When you run out of Wish charges, it's still a +1 luck sword.

**Dagger of Venom** (Rare): +1 dagger. Once per day, coat the blade — next hit deals extra 2d10 poison, DC 15 CON save or poisoned for 1 minute.

## Armor

### +X Armor
- **+1 Armor** (Rare): AC bonus on top of the armor's base.
- **+2 Armor** (Very Rare): +2 AC.
- **+3 Armor** (Legendary): +3 AC.

### Notable Armor (SRD)

**Adamantine Armor** (Uncommon): Any critical hit against you becomes a normal hit. Works on medium or heavy armor. Cheap and effective.

**Mithral Armor** (Uncommon): Medium or heavy armor without the STR requirement and stealth disadvantage is removed. Mithral half plate is 15 + DEX (max 2) with no stealth penalty. Mithral full plate is AC 18 with no STR requirement or stealth penalty.

**Armor of Resistance** (Rare, attunement): Resistance to one damage type (specified when found).

**Dragon Scale Mail** (Very Rare, attunement): Scale mail (AC 14 + DEX max 2) with no stealth disadvantage. Resistance to the dragon type's damage. Advantage on saves vs. Frightful Presence.

**Plate Armor of Etherealness** (Legendary, attunement): Plate armor. Action to cast Etherealness once per long rest.

## Shields

**Shield +1/+2/+3**: Additional AC on top of the shield's base +2.

**Shield of Missile Attraction** (Rare, attunement, cursed): +2 AC. Resistance to ranged weapon attacks. Curse: ranged attacks targeting creatures within 10 ft of you target you instead.

**Animated Shield** (Very Rare, attunement): Bonus action to animate — hovers near you granting +2 AC with no hand required. Lasts 1 minute or until you dismiss it, drop it, or are incapacitated.

**Spellguard Shield** (Very Rare, attunement): Advantage on saving throws against spells and magical effects. Spell attacks have disadvantage against you.

## Wondrous Items

### Uncommon

**Bag of Holding** (no attunement): Interior is 64 cubic feet, 500 lb capacity. Always weighs 15 lb. Breathing: 10 minutes of air divided by number of creatures. Placing inside another extradimensional space (like a Portable Hole) opens a gate to the Astral Plane and destroys both items.

**Boots of Elvenkind** (no attunement): Advantage on Stealth checks to move silently.

**Cloak of Elvenkind** (attunement): Disadvantage on Perception checks to see you. Advantage on Stealth checks to hide.

**Goggles of Night** (no attunement): Darkvision 60 ft (or extend existing by 60 ft).

**Gauntlets of Ogre Power** (attunement): STR becomes 19. No effect if STR is already 19+.

**Headband of Intellect** (attunement): INT becomes 19.

**Pearl of Power** (attunement by spellcaster): Once per day, recover one expended spell slot of 3rd level or lower.

**Immovable Rod** (no attunement): Press button to fix in place. Holds 8,000 lb. DC 30 STR to move. Endlessly creative uses.

### Rare

**Cloak of Displacement** (attunement): Attack rolls against you have disadvantage. If hit, this stops working until start of your next turn. Also stops if restrained or speed 0.

**Ring of Protection** (attunement): +1 AC and saving throws.

**Cloak of Protection** (attunement): +1 AC and saving throws. (Same as Ring of Protection but different item slot.)

**Amulet of Health** (attunement): CON becomes 19.

**Belt of Giant Strength** (attunement): Sets STR to a specific score based on the belt type. Hill (21), Stone/Frost (23), Fire (25), Cloud (27), Storm (29). Ranges from rare to legendary.

**Periapt of Wound Closure** (attunement): Stabilize automatically at 0 HP. Double HP regained from spending Hit Dice.

**Rope of Climbing** (no attunement): 60 ft rope. Command it to move, knot, unknot, coil, animate. AC 20, 20 HP.

### Very Rare

**Cloak of Invisibility** (attunement): Pull the hood up to become invisible. 2 hours of use per day (1-minute increments).

**Manual of Bodily Health / Quickness of Action / etc.** (no attunement, consumed): Increase an ability score and its maximum by 2. One-time use (the book crumbles).

**Ring of Regeneration** (attunement): Regain 1d6 HP every 10 minutes if you have at least 1 HP. Regrow lost body parts over 1d6+1 days.

**Staff of Power** (attunement by sorcerer, warlock, wizard): +2 to attack, damage, AC, saving throws, and spell attack rolls. 20 charges, recharges 2d8+4 per dawn. Can cast various spells using charges. Retributive Strike option on breaking.

### Legendary

**Holy Avenger** (legendary, attunement by paladin): +3 weapon. Extra 2d10 radiant vs. fiends and undead. While held, allies within 10 ft have advantage on saves against spells and magical effects. The paladin's dream weapon.

**Staff of the Magi** (legendary, attunement by sorcerer, warlock, wizard): +2 to spell attacks. 50 charges. Advantage on saves vs. spells. Absorb spells targeted at you to regain charges. Can cast many high-level spells. Retributive Strike on breaking can be a TPK-level explosion.

**Robe of the Archmagi** (legendary, attunement by sorcerer, warlock, wizard): AC 15 + DEX. Advantage on saves vs. spells and magical effects. Spell save DC and spell attack bonus each +2.

## Cursed Items

Some magic items carry curses that aren't revealed by Identify or short rest examination. The curse activates when the item is used or attuned. Common curse effects:
- Can't unattune or remove the item without Remove Curse
- Personality changes
- Disadvantage on certain rolls
- Attracts enemies
- Deals damage to the wielder under certain conditions

Cursed items are a narrative tool. They work best when the downside creates interesting decisions, not just penalties.
